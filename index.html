Scientist Biographies

1. Sir C. V. Raman (1888–1970)

  • Field: Physics
  • Achievement: Discovered the Raman Effect, which describes how light changes its wavelength when it passes through a transparent material.
  • Significance: This discovery was key in understanding the molecular and atomic structure of materials.
  • Award: Won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1930—making him the first Asian to receive a Nobel Prize in any branch of science.
  • Legacy: National Science Day is celebrated on February 28th in India to honor his discovery (1928).

2. Homi Jehangir Bhabha (1909–1966)

  • Field: Nuclear Physics
  • Achievement: Known as the Father of India’s Nuclear Program.
  • Contributions:
    • Founded the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR).
    • Played a pivotal role in developing nuclear energy for peaceful purposes in India.
  • Legacy: The Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC) in Mumbai is named in his honor.

3. Vikram Sarabhai (1919–1971)

  • Field: Space Science
  • Achievement: Known as the Father of the Indian Space Program.
  • Contributions:
    • Founded ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) in 1969.
    • Initiated India’s first satellite project Aryabhata (launched in 1975 after his death).
  • Legacy: The Vikram lander in the Chandrayaan missions is named after him.

4. Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015)

  • Field: Aerospace Engineering, Defense Science
  • Achievement: Played a leading role in India’s missile development (Agni, Prithvi) and nuclear weapons tests in 1998.
  • Positions Held:
    • Chief Scientific Adviser to the Government of India.
    • 11th President of India (2002–2007), known as the "People’s President".
  • Legacy: Remembered as the Missile Man of India for his visionary role in defense and science education.

5. Jagadish Chandra Bose (1858–1937)

  • Field: Physics and Plant Biology
  • Achievements:
    • Demonstrated that plants have life and can respond to stimuli.
    • Pioneered research in radio waves—built a device similar to a modern radio detector.
  • Firsts:
    • One of the first to demonstrate wireless communication.
    • Among the earliest scientists to work across disciplines (biophysics).
  • Legacy: A true polymath, he is often referred to as the Father of Modern Science in India.

6. Satyendra Nath Bose (1894–1974)

  • Field: Theoretical Physics
  • Achievement: Known for Bose-Einstein statistics, in collaboration with Albert Einstein.
  • Contributions:
    • Provided foundational work for quantum mechanics.
    • The particle “Boson” is named after him (bosons are one of the two fundamental classes of particles in the universe).
  • Legacy: His work is still central in particle physics and quantum theory.

7. Meghnad Saha (1893–1956)

  • Field: Astrophysics
  • Achievement: Developed the Saha Ionization Equation, which helps explain the physical and chemical conditions of stars.
  • Impact: His work is crucial in stellar astrophysics, especially in determining the temperature and composition of stars.
  • Legacy: Also involved in planning India's science policy and industrialization post-independence.

8. Venkatraman Ramakrishnan (b. 1952)

  • Field: Structural Biology
  • Achievement: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry (2009) for his work on the structure and function of the ribosome.
  • Significance:
    • His research explains how cells make proteins—vital in medicine and genetics.
    • Shared the prize with Thomas Steitz and Ada Yonath.
  • Legacy: He was the President of the Royal Society (UK) from 2015–2020.

9. Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887–1920)

  • Field: Pure Mathematics Key
  • Achievements:
    • Created nearly 3,900 original theorems and identities—many of which were years ahead of their time.
    • Famous for Ramanujan’s infinite series, mock theta functions, and contributions to partition theory.
  • Collaborations: Worked with G.H. Hardy at Cambridge University, who considered him a genius on par with Euler and Jacobi.
  • Legacy:
    • Died young at 32, but left behind a mathematical legacy that influences number theory and string theory today.
    • Honored through books, films ("The Man Who Knew Infinity"), and National Mathematics Day (Dec 22) in India.

10. Salim Ali (1896–1987)

  • Field: Ornithology (Bird Science)
  • Achievements:
    • Conducted the first systematic bird surveys in India.
    • Wrote "The Book of Indian Birds", a classic reference.
  • Impact:
    • Popularized birdwatching in India.
    • Played a key role in the creation of bird sanctuaries and wildlife conservation efforts.
  • Legacy:
    • Known as the Birdman of India.
    • Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary in Goa and other reserves are named in his honor.

11. Dr. Har Gobind Khorana (1922–2011)

  • Field: Genetics and Biochemistry
  • Achievements:
    • Shared the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine (1968) for work on the genetic code and protein synthesis.
    • First to synthesize an artificial gene in a lab (a huge breakthrough in genetic engineering).
  • Legacy:
    • Though he became a U.S. citizen, he was born in British India (now in Pakistan).
    • Helped lay the foundation for biotechnology and genetic research.
    • The Khorana Program fosters scientific collaboration between India and the U.S.

12. Birbal Sahni (1891–1949)

  • Field: Paleobotany (study of fossil plants)
  • Achievements:
    • Founded the Birbal Sahni Institute of Palaeosciences, a premier institute in fossil research.
    • Made significant contributions to understanding India’s prehistoric flora.
  • Legacy:
    • Known for integrating botany and geology to study plant evolution.
    • Helped promote science education in post-independence India.

13. S. S. Abhyankar (1930–2012)

  • Field: Algebraic Geometry and Commutative Algebra
  • Achievements:
    • Known for Abhyankar’s conjectures in algebra and contributions to algebraic surfaces and singularity theory.
    • Worked on resolution of singularities, which is fundamental in modern geometry.
  • Legacy:
    • Professor at Purdue University.
    • One of the prominent Indian-born mathematicians in the West who influenced modern mathematics.

14. Asima Chatterjee (1917–2006)

  • Field: Organic Chemistry and Phytomedicine
  • Achievements:
    • Pioneered research on anti-epileptic and anti-malarial drugs from Indian plants.
    • First woman to earn a Doctorate in Science (D.Sc.) from an Indian university.
  • Legacy:
    • Her work in natural products chemistry is globally respected.
    • Promoted women in STEM in a male-dominated era.

15. G. N. Ramachandran (1922–2001)

  • Field: Structural Biology and Physics
  • Achievements: Developed the Ramachandran Plot, a fundamental tool for understanding protein structure in molecular biology.
  • Legacy:
    • His research laid the groundwork for protein folding and biomolecular structure analysis.
    • Revered globally in molecular biology and biophysics.

16. Raj Reddy (b. 1937)

  • Field: Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence
  • Achievements:
    • One of the early pioneers of AI.
    • Focused on human-computer interaction and AI for developing nations.
  • Legacy:
    • Won the Turing Award in 1994 (highest honor in computer science).
    • Founded institutions to promote tech in rural and underdeveloped areas.

17. M. S. Swaminathan (1925–2023)

  • Field: Agricultural Science
  • Achievements:
    • Architect of India’s Green Revolution.
    • Introduced high-yield crop varieties and modern farming techniques.
  • Legacy:
    • elped make India self-sufficient in food production.
    • Known as the Father of Economic Botany in India.
    • Advocated sustainable farming and food security.
